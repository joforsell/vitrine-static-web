Sure... but what is it really?

The app your paint desk is missing

Your collection, organised

Catalogue every miniature you own, whether it's assembled, primed, half-painted, or finished. Track where each one sits in your pipeline so the pile of shame doesn't feel quite so shameful.

Paint recipes that stick around

Record exactly how you painted something. Every layer, wash, and drybrush, saved so you can recreate it six months later without guessing. Attach recipes to miniatures and reuse them across projects.

Steal recipes from painters you admire

See someone's incredible NMM gold or perfect skin tone? If they've shared the recipe, you can save it to your own collection and use it on your next project. No more screenshotting Reddit tutorials.

Painting sessions that guide you

Start a painting session on any miniature, with or without a recipe. Follow it step by step, skip what doesn't apply, take notes on what you changed. Building a recipe as you go? The session captures it for you so you can reuse it next time.

Show off your work

When a mini is done, put it in your showcase. Share it with other painters or keep it as a personal gallery of everything you've finished.

What makes it different?

Vitrine is built specifically for miniature painters. Not a general hobby tracker, not a social media feed with a paint filter. Every feature is designed around how painters actually work: by project, by recipe, by step.
