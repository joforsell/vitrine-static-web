Inside Vitrine

Everything you do with your miniatures

Catalogue every model. Save the recipes you write. Track the paints on your shelf. Show your work off in your own public showroom or browse what other painters are doing. And watch your hobby take shape in the Statistics hub.

Miniatures

Catalogue every model. Track each piece individually as it moves through your pipeline: unboxed, assembled, primed, work-in-progress, painted, based, finished, or whatever stages match how you actually paint. Each one is yours to rename, reorder, or remove. A 5-pack squad can sit at 2 finished, 1 wip, and 2 primed at the same time. The pile of shame finally gets honest accounting.

  • Add as many photos as you want per mini. Drag to reorder, and pick one as the hero shot at the top of the detail screen
  • Multiple named collections like Space Marines, Shelf of Shame, or Ongoing Commissions
  • Tag every mini with manufacturer, setting, model, or genre. Use those tags (plus status, scale, and date) to filter and search across both your own collection and the Discover community feed
  • Link to the 3D print model on MyMiniFactory, Thingiverse, or Printables, for your own reference or for others to see in your showroom
  • Show them off: every named account gets a handle. Use it to share your public showroom inside the app, or as app.vitrineminis.com/u/your-handle anywhere else
  • Browse the community: get inspired by others' showcased miniatures in a chronological feed

Paint recipes & session mode

Record exactly how you painted something. Every layer, wash, drybrush, glaze, saved so you can recreate it six months later without guessing. Recipes are made to be shared: pull anyone else's into your library, or publish yours so another painter can use it. When it's time to paint, start a Session and follow any recipe step by step.

  • Structured steps: paint, technique, freetext notes, plus a colour swatch for every paint used
  • Mark a recipe as a template and create new ones pre-populated with its steps, paints, cover image, and step photos
  • Guided sessions walk you through a saved recipe; freeform sessions record what you do as you do it, and can be saved as a recipe at the end
  • Sessions persist between visits, resuming exactly where you left off
  • "Show Alternatives" on any step suggests cross-brand substitutes sorted by colour distance, and highlights the ones you already own
  • Mix ratio per step, version history, and step-level WIP photos for the moments you want to document a recipe in detail
  • Search the community: find public recipes by name or model tag, then save any to your own library

Paint tracking

Your paint cabinet, on your phone. Vitrine knows what you own and how every paint relates to every other across brands, so you always have a substitute lined up when your usual one runs out.

  • Personal collection of every paint you own
  • Cross-brand equivalents from community conversion charts. Aliases for renamed paints (Goblin Green resolves to Warboss Green automatically)
  • "Add Entire Range" bulk-adds a whole brand+range to your collection with a count preview
  • Import from PaintRack to bring an existing inventory across in one go
  • Scan paint barcodes to find them in the catalog
  • Sort by colour similarity to find alternates by eye
  • Submit paints that aren't in the catalog yet, and track review status in My Submissions
  • Share your library with a partner or small group via invite link, and see merged paint availability

Statistics

Every painted piece, every stage transition, every session feeds a Statistics hub that turns your pipeline into shareable infographics. Honest, motivating, and just a bit competitive with your past self.

  • State Timeline — pieces moving through stages over a date range you pick. Export as an animated video to share progress
  • Pile of Shame — counts and trends for unstarted and wip miniatures. Honest, unflinching, slightly motivating
  • Collection Comparison — completion percentage by collection. Which army is closest to done?
  • Time in State — how long pieces sit at each stage. Find the bottleneck in your hobby
  • Completion Rate — finished pieces vs total over time
  • Painting Velocity — pieces finished per week or per month. Set a pace and beat it
  • Every chart exports as a branded infographic, ready to post wherever you share your hobby
